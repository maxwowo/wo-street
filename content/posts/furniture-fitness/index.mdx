Day nine of quarantine, I can physically feel the gains slipping away from me, which is why I've been experimenting with doing exercising using the furniture in my hotel room. So here's a review of how useful each type of furniture is as make-shift gym equipments.

## Lamps

Excellent pieces of furniture for our purposes. You can use floor lamps for bench press or shoulder press. The main problem is that they can be a bit too light to bench press, so you're better off just doing the good old push ups. They're also often more heavier on the bottom end, since that's the part that sits on the floor and stabilizes the lamp, but you can always grip them slightly away from the center of mass towards the bottom to balance the torques out. If you don't have floor lamps at your disposal, you can always just use table lamps as dumbbells to substitute.

## Chairs

Comfortable to sit in, but not so comfortable to lift. Their bulkiness makes them difficult to grip since you can only really hold them by the legs, which are not strong enough to support the weight of the entire chair being swung around. They also significantly shift your center of mass towards the front, which will cause you to lose balance, fall over, and die.

## Floor

Very reliable and easily accessible. Great for all sorts of exercises, like planking, bicycle crunches, push ups, and what not. They can get a bit dirty though so it's recommended to vacuum or mop them beforehand.
