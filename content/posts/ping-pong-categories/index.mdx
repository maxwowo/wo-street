Over the past couple of months I managed to develop a world-class ping pong serve, which makes use of ancient forbidden techniques never seen before in the history of modern table tennis to produce an ungodly amount of spin on the ball, making it curve around the fabrics of space-time and causality itself, confusing the opponent and rendering counterplays next to impossible to execute.

Here's how the serve works. Position yourself to the right of the table, place the bat to the left of your butt and hold the ball very close to it with your left hand. That's for right-handed people though and if you're left-handed then maybe invert it or something ey. If you do this right you should feel like you're holding a pistol and are about to pull it out and pop off. When you're ready to execute the serve, chuck the ball up, but not too high so you don't give the opponent the time to react, and slice the ball on the right from low to high like you're playing fruit ninja. If you manage to pull it off perfectly then the ball should receive so much side spin that it will disappear into a parallel universe for a brief but observable moment. If this happens then you will officially transcend humanity and truly become one with the universe.

With that said, this serve still has one counterplay, although it's extremely difficult to pull it off. All you need to do as the receiver is to hit it incredibly hard with a top spin, which somehow cancels out the side spin and returns the serve with a shit ton of top spin. I still don't exactly know why a top spin can cancel out a side spin, but it eez what it eez.

So what exactly is ping pong categories? It's not actually related to my serve, I just wanted to show it off. Basically ping pong categories is when you play ping pong, but before you hit each shot you have to say a noun that belongs to a specific category, e.g. for the category "aquatic creatures" you might want to say phytoplankton or something. The only rule is that you can't say what's already been said. It may sound easy but it's actually hard AF, so it's not advisable to choose obscure categories like "countries with a moon in their flag."
