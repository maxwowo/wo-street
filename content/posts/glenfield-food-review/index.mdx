A friend lives in an area in Auckland called Glenfield and we visit him a lot, mostly uninvited, but he doesn't mind so it's all good. We often get food outside when we go to his place as well, so here's a review of some of the restaurants in Glenfield.

## Tanpopo

The OG ramen shop near the bus stop. Decent portion size for a $15 meal with good soup, but the noodles always tasted like instant ramen noodles so that's kind of a scam. Who knows, maybe that's just how noodles are supposed to taste like. It's recommended to get the classic ramen, which should be the first option on the menu, since everything else is either too salty or has too much random stuff chucked on top of it. You should also get the chicken karaage alongside the ramen as well since the ramen doesn't come with enough meat, which is pretty important to make those gains.

## Maccas

The classic Ronald McDonald with drive through. Pretty close to Tanpopo so we often go there for an ice cream after ramen. Portion sizes and pricing are typical for a Maccas, which is not surprising at all. Beef burgers taste significantly better than Maccas in kangaroo land, probably because cows in NZ have more space to roam around so are more thicc. Worth noting that the drive through is guaranteed to get at least one item wrong for every order without fail, so if you're the type of person to hit "I'm feeling lucky" on Google, then this is definitely something to try out.

## Mall Maccas

The better Maccas. Doesn't offer drive through but gives more pickles for burgers so you're definitely getting more bang for your buck.
