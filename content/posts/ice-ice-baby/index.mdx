I had a beer with this dude that's apparently pretty big in the NZ startup scene after co-hosting a hackaton with a student society at Auckland University during my time back in NZ. He was one of the judges and so got stay behind a little bit after judging the shit out of everyone that participated. We talked a bit about startups, startups that he's investing in, and most importantly taking cold showers. I forgot exactly why we were talking about that, but he mentioned that he sleeps four hours a day and takes cold showers both immediately after waking up and before going to bed. In winter.

That's pretty insane, so I did some spicy research and found out about this thing called the [Wim Hof Method](https://www.wimhofmethod.com), which is basically just some random dude doing icy stunts, like meditating in Antarctica or something, at least that's what he seems to be doing in the picture on the home page. Anyway, it feels like a scam since the page pushes his classes like crazy. But upon closer inspection, I found a page that lists several benefits of doing icy things, e.g. more energy, better sleep, and better immune system, which is pretty important these days to defend yourself against the Novel Coronavirus Disease Nineteen.

So I decided to try it out myself. I've been taking cold showers everyday for like a month now, and honestly, I don't notice any difference. I guess it does save some electricity though since less water needs to be heated up, so at least I'm saving some money.

I might just keep doing it though since it became more of a habit now so it'll be a bit weird to change it up.
