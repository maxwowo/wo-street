I was originally gonna title this post "spa review", but then I realized it'll be more entertaining to talk about some dead skin cells instead, so you're gonna have to bear with me on this one.

So a couple weeks ago the gang and I drove to a place in NZ famous for its hot spring spas called Rotorua. Fun fact about the place, its spas are so full of minerals that the entire town constantly smells like shit, which is a pretty unfortunate side effect. Had to wake up really early to pick up the others but it wasn't too bad to drive there since I got to blast rock music, which was so fast-paced that it made the drive feel shorter. We also stopped by an ice cream shop on the way there, which was absolutely necessary because apparently they make the best ice cream in all of NZ. The ice cream tasted just like ice cream though, nothing special, so it gets a 8/10.

Anyway so we arrived and ate some stuff, then went and rode on a luge down a mountain several times. The luge went fast AF so it basically felt like

<Youtube src='https://www.youtube.com/embed/_--BVXf8loc' />

Fuck yeah I was actually a beast on the luge.

And then we went to the spa. We went to a place called Polynesian Spa which is where all the typical tourists go, but that's basically us so it's all good. There were several pools and we tried out all of them to make the trip worth its money. All the pools had different temperatures and the one that we're focusing on today is the hottest pool, which also happened to be the last one we tried. It was so hot that it had white dust-looking particles in it. Although the signs did say that there might be random things in the water because of the minerals, we were absolutely convinced that it was bullshit and that they had to be dead skin cells shed by 80 year old grandpas. They just had to be.

But yeah we stayed in the pool for 10 minutes anyway hanging out with dead skin cells because we had to stay in each pool for at least 10 minutes to thoroughly try it out, and we for sure weren't gonna give up on the last pool.

That was kinda gross though, and I still think about it when I lay awake at night sometimes.
